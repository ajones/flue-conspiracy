## Article excerpt

Cholinesterase inhibitors remain among the most widely prescribed Alzheimer's drugs because of their established efficacy and affordability. These medications increase acetylcholine levels in the brain, helping improve memory and cognitive function in patients with mild to moderate Alzheimer's disease.

Drugs such as donepezil, rivastigmine, and galantamine continue playing a central role in treatment, especially in markets where newer biologic therapies are less accessible due to cost limitations.

Donepezil maintains a particularly strong position because of its effectiveness, once-daily dosage, and availability in generic form. The drug remains a preferred first-line treatment in many countries because of its affordability and clinical familiarity.

Galantamine also remains important because of its dual mechanism of action and availability in extended-release formulations designed to improve patient convenience and adherence.

Eisai and Biogen secured FDA approval for LEQEMBI IQLIK in August 2025, introducing a subcutaneous autoinjector maintenance therapy for Alzheimer’s disease.
