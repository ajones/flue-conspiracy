# Gerontologist on dementia caregiving and burnout

Paraphrased extract of the HELLO! article. The live page was accessible, so this note captures the main points rather than a verbatim copy.

## Details

- Dr Javier Yanguas, a psychologist and gerontologist, wrote about caregiving after personally caring for his mother.
- He says there is a major gap between advising caregivers in theory and actually doing the work in real life.
- The piece frames caregiving as deeply transformative, but also physically and emotionally exhausting.
- He describes the practical burden of helping with washing, dressing, eating, and coping with behavioral changes such as irritability and wandering.
- He also emphasizes the emotional load: guilt, ambivalence, communication loss, and putting one's own life plans on hold.
- The article argues that care is undervalued and still too often treated as women's work.
- His advice is to seek support, avoid doing it alone, balance caregiving with the rest of life, take breaks when needed, and be gentle with oneself.
- The article closes with a call for a more realistic view of ageing and better support for older adults and caregivers.
