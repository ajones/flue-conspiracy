# Sundowning

It's called sundowning, one of the most common and misunderstood behaviors in dementia. As the sun goes down, the dementia brain becomes overwhelmed, and agitation, confusion, pacing, and wanting to "go home" — even when already home — often spike in the late afternoon and early evening. It's not the person being difficult; the brain is literally processing light differently, and the shift from daylight to dusk can become overstimulating.

Closing the blinds before 4pm can help cut off the visual trigger before it starts. Turn on warm lamps, keep the TV off or low, and make the environment calm before the brain has a chance to spiral. This simple change has helped more caregivers than almost any medication prescribed for it.
