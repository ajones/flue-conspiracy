# Bill Colorado Travel Notes

Use this file to organize Colorado-trip behavior into repeatable categories so we can compare trips and pull out practical strategies.

## How To Read This File

- Record each trip in the same structure.
- Capture what changed first, what escalated next, and what helped.
- Keep observations factual and specific.
- Update the strategy section only when something clearly worked.

## Colorado Baseline

- Colorado is a different living setup from Texas.
- The environment tends to be more social, with more golf and outdoor activity.
- The living space is generally serene and has very few day-to-day demands.
- Bill is still expected to walk the dog in the morning because the exercise helps both him and the dog.
- Once he settles after arrival, Bill should generally be calmer in Colorado than during the travel transition.

## Trip Phases

### Pre-Trip / Packing

- Routine disruption can make Bill more combative and disagreement-prone.
- He may resist being directed and can react strongly to decisions about who drives.
- He can become overly focused on logistics, control, or the exact sequence of leaving.

### Drive

- He may be extremely talkative and read signs, billboards, and car colors.
- He can become highly engaged in technical or concrete topics if something in the environment sparks it.
- He may not want to do Sudoku or other quiet activities during the drive.

### Arrival / New House

- A change in routine and a house he has not been to recently can trigger stress.
- He may not remember how to do ordinary maintenance tasks.
- Coaching can offend him even when he needs the help.
- This is usually the point where he should start calming down once he has arrived and settled in.

### Evening / Wind-Down

- TV distraction has helped after a stressful afternoon.
- Dinner and a lower-demand evening appear to reduce tension after the day has gone sideways.

## Patterns To Watch

- Control issues around who drives or how the trip is managed.
- Increased talkativeness and repetitive commentary during long drives.
- Stress when arriving at unfamiliar or less familiar places.
- Settle-in days can still be yellow even when Colorado is generally calmer once arrival is complete.
- Offense when corrected or coached during practical tasks.
- Better settling when the evening becomes low-demand and distracting.

## Strategy Ideas To Test

- Keep departure decisions simple and settled early.
- Expect more talkative, less flexible behavior on the drive.
- Treat Colorado as a lower-demand environment once arrival is over.
- Expect a short settle-in period if something is not working right.
- Minimize coaching during arrival tasks; offer brief, calm prompts only when needed.
- Use dinner and TV as a decompression sequence after a hard arrival day.
- Track whether familiarity, routine, or lower demands reduce the next-day carryover.
