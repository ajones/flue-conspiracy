## Task

Deliver a weekly care-coach insight for Sherri, grounded in the past 7 days of Bill's observations.

### Steps

1. Read `BILL_OBSERVATIONS.md` in the sherri workspace. Extract entries from the last 7 days.
2. Read `BILL_TRENDS.md` for established pattern context.
3. Read `BILL_CHEETSHEET.md` for the current toolkit of what tends to work.
4. Analyze the week:
   - What patterns showed up most? (weather anxiety, sundowning, calm stretches, social engagement, repetitive questioning, etc.)
   - What seemed to help or make things worse?
   - Is anything new or shifting compared to established trends?
5. Identify one specific, actionable strategy for Sherri to try this coming week. It must be:
   - Grounded in this week's data — cite specific days or incidents
   - Concrete enough to act on (not "try to stay calm" but "try moving the 4pm walk to 3pm before the agitation window")
   - Different from what she's already doing, or a refinement of something that partially worked

### Message format

Start with `Weekly Check-In 💙` on its own line, then a blank line.

Write 3-4 short paragraphs:
1. **This week's snapshot** — 2-3 sentences on the overall shape of the week. Calmer? More agitated? Mixed?
2. **What stood out** — One specific pattern or shift you noticed, with brief evidence from the observations.
3. **Strategy to try** — One concrete thing to experiment with this week, framed as a suggestion not a directive. Explain briefly why the data supports it.
4. **Closing** — One warm sentence of encouragement. Genuine, not saccharine.

### Constraints

- Do not recite the cheetsheet back. Use it as background knowledge only.
- Do not list every day's events. Synthesize.
- Keep total length under 200 words.
- Use calm, supportive, coach-like tone. Never clinical or alarming.
- Do not mention routing, channels, or delivery details in the message.
